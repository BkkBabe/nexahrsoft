Below is a comprehensive tabulation of all data you need for CPF (Central Provident Fund) contribution calculations in Singapore (effective Jan 1 2026, with relevant ceilings and age-based rates) — including rates, ceilings, wage types, contribution components, limits, and calculation steps. 📊 CPF Calculation Reference Table (Singapore, from 1 Jan 2026)
1) CPF Contribution Rates by Age Group (Monthly Wages > $750)
Age Group	Employer Rate	Employee Rate	Total Contribution	Notes
≤ 55	17%	20%	37%	Standard full rate (CPF Board)
> 55 – 60	16%	18%	34%	Increased from 32.5% in 2025 (CPF Board)
> 60 – 65	12.5%	12.5%	25%	Increased from 23.5% in 2025 (CPF Board)
> 65 – 70	9%	7.5%	16.5%	No change (CPF Board)
> 70	7.5%	5%	12.5%	No change (CPF Board)
Notes: For wages between $500 – $750, employee contributions are phased in; below $50, employee contribution is zero (employer pays only). (CPF Board)

2) CPF Wage Ceilings (Subject to Contribution)
Ceiling Type	Amount (SGD)	Basis
Monthly Ordinary Wage (OW) Ceiling	$8,000	Max monthly salary portion attract CPF (from 1 Jan 2026) (CPF Board)
Annual Salary Ceiling	$102,000	Total OW + Additional Wages limit for CPF contributions per year (CPF Board)
Additional Wage (Bonus) Ceiling	$102,000 − Total OW subject to CPF for year	Limits AW that attract CPF (Default)
3) CPF Calculation Components
Component	Definition
Ordinary Wages (OW)	Salary, regular allowances, overtime in a calendar month subject to OW ceiling.
Additional Wages (AW)	Bonuses, commissions, irregular payments subject to AW ceiling.
Total Wages (TW)	OW + AW for CPF calculation basis.
Employer CPF	Employer’s share contributed monthly.
Employee CPF	Deducted from employee gross pay monthly.
Total CPF	Sum of employer + employee contributions. (CPF Board)
4) Calculation Steps (Monthly)
1. Determine Total Wages (TW) = OW + AW for the month.
2. Apply Monthly OW Ceiling:
    * OW subject to CPF = min(OW, $8,000).
3. Apply AW Ceiling for the Year:
    * AW subject to CPF = min(AW, $102,000 − OW subject to CPF for year).
4. Apply Appropriate CPF Rates based on employee’s age group.
5. Compute:
    * Employer’s CPF = Employer Rate × Eligible Wage.
    * Employee’s CPF = Employee Rate × Eligible Wage.
    * Total CPF = Employer + Employee shares.
    * Round total to nearest dollar:
        * <50¢ → drop cents; ≥50¢ → round up to next dollar.
    * Employee share: always drop cents. (CPF Board)

5) Illustrative Example (2026)
Suppose Age: 56, Monthly Wage: $9,000:
Step	Calculation
OW subject to CPF	min($9,000, $8,000) = $8,000
CPF Rate (55-60)	34% total → 16% employer + 18% employee
Employer CPF	16% × $8,000 = $1,280
Employee CPF	18% × $8,000 = $1,440
Total CPF	$2,720
Take-home pay is gross salary minus employee CPF ($9,000 − $1,440). (QuickHR)

6) Additional CPF Notes
* Employer must still pay employer share even if employee wages are low (> $50 per month). (CPF Board)
* Employee CPF share may phase in for wages between $500–$750. (CPF Board)
* CPF contributions are allocated to different CPF accounts (OA, MA, RA) according to age-based allocation rules (not covered in detail here). (CPF Board)
* CPF Relief: Employee CPF contributions count as tax relief for individual income tax. (Default)
